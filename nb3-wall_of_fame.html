CoRA — Corporate Remote Assistant
Barnabas Berez

CoRA rethinks what remote work support means. Using an object-detection model to identify a phone-like object, CoRA monitors for distraction — and when the threshold is crossed, politely asks if the user wants a break. If yes, a servo-powered wooden peg physically presses a key on the keyboard: an analogue, slightly mischievous way to keep the user "online" while they rest. CoRA can also play football, commentate a match, and cheer.

Experimental Neuroscience Bootcamp September 2025
Person-following robot with voice command.
Ziyue Liu

A robot that follows people using an object-detection model, tracking movement in real time. When a person comes close, it raises an alert and switches into voice-command mode — listening for "left" or "right" and executing a clean turn on command. A complete perception-to-action loop built from scratch in three weeks.

Experimental Neuroscience Bootcamp September 2025
Wall-E Learns to Fetch — a robot with place cells.
Barnopriyo Dutta & Jake Laherty

Inspired by Pixar's Wall-E, this robot recognises objects with a convolutional neural net, moves toward targets using visual features for depth sensing, scoops items into an internal chamber, and navigates HOME by path-integrating from those same visual features. A smartphone served as both camera and gyroscope — combining real-time vision with spatial feedback for a full perception-to-action loop, built from scratch in under three weeks.
