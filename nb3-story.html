Our story

From training
neuroscientists to
teaching the next generation.

For over 15 years, we taught advanced technologies to scientists and researchers all over the world. Then we realised something: most people — especially our kids — never get the chance to truly understand how modern technology works. So we set out to change that.

Where we started.
Teaching the
world's best scientists.

For more than a decade we trained Master's and PhD students at leading international institutions. The curriculum was demanding, hands-on, and built on one belief: you only truly understand something when you build it yourself.

What we realised.
This shouldn't be
only for a few.

Everything we taught to elite researchers — how circuits work, how computers think, how AI sees — is knowledge that everyone deserves. The gap between what experts know and what everyone else is taught is not inevitable. It's a choice. We chose differently.

What we built.
A complete system,
open to anyone.

We took everything learned over 15 years of training scientists and rebuilt it from the ground up — accessible, hands-on, and connected to real technology. What was once taught only to a few is now available to anyone, anywhere.

How it started — and how it evolved

A curriculum
fifteen years in the making.

Not a startup idea. Not a weekend project. The No Black Boxes curriculum grew from many years of teaching some of the world's most advanced learners — and learning, every time, how to make it better.

2010
2010 — 2021
Training scientists
at the highest level.

For more than a decade, the No Black Boxes team developed and delivered training materials for Master's and PhD students at leading international institutions. These courses focused on advanced technologies in neuroscience and experimental science — hands-on, from first principles, nothing hidden.

2020
2020 — 2024
Scaling beyond
elite training.

We began developing a new curriculum to offer courses to anyone, anywhere — curious learners who want to understand how the technology they use actually works. The first online course launched during the COVID-19 pandemic together with the Cajal Programme. Since then, we have trained over 2,000 students from more than 60 countries worldwide.

2025
2025
Becoming part of the
future of education.

We began adapting the curriculum to create engaging material for secondary-school students. This became the Build a Brain course at the new London AI Campus — first taught in London schools, and now available to anyone. The NB3 robot was designed specifically for this course: a complete, hands-on system that takes learners from a single circuit to a working AI.

The founder

Adam Kampff.
The vision behind it all.

Over more than 15 years, Adam Kampff — a neuroscientist — developed a new educational approach combining neuroscience, electronics, computing, and hands-on learning to teach how modern technology really works.

Over the years, this approach was shaped, expanded, and refined through collaboration with students, educators, and researchers who worked with him and were influenced by his ideas. The parallel he drew between understanding the brain and understanding technology became the foundation of everything No Black Boxes teaches.

"You only truly understand something when you can build it yourself — and when you can see, clearly, every layer beneath it."

The No Black Boxes Foundation
Carrying the vision forward.

Following Adam's death, the No Black Boxes Foundation was established to preserve, develop, and scale his approach — so it can reach future generations of learners. Guided by a board of trustees and a growing team of educators, researchers, and collaborators, the Foundation ensures that high-quality technology education remains accessible to everyone, without barriers.
